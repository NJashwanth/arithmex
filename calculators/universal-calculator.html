What Is a Scientific Calculator?

A scientific calculator goes beyond basic arithmetic by supporting advanced operations such as trigonometric functions (sine, cosine, tangent), logarithms, exponents, square roots, and factorials. Scientists, engineers, students, and professionals rely on scientific calculators daily to solve complex equations quickly and accurately.

Unlike standard four-function calculators, a scientific calculator follows the standard mathematical order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS), meaning expressions like 2 + 3 × 4 correctly evaluate to 14, not 20. This ensures reliable results for nested and complex calculations.

How to Use This Calculator

Enter your expression using the on-screen buttons or your keyboard. The calculator supports parentheses for grouping, the π constant, and scientific functions accessible through the function buttons. Press = or hit Enter to evaluate the expression.

  • Trigonometric functions: sin, cos, and tan operate in degrees by default. Enter the angle and press the function button.
  • Logarithms: Use log for base-10 logarithms and ln for natural logarithms (base e).
  • Exponents: Use the xⁿ button or type ** for power operations. For example, 2 ** 3 gives 8.
  • Square root: Press the √ button followed by the number, or use parentheses for complex expressions like √(16 + 9).
  • History: Every calculation is saved to your browser's local storage. Review, copy, or clear past results anytime.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this calculator accurate for homework and exams?

Yes. This calculator uses JavaScript's built-in Math library, which implements IEEE 754 double-precision floating-point arithmetic — the same standard used by scientific software and most desktop calculators. Results are accurate to approximately 15 significant digits.

Does the calculator save my data?

Your calculation history is stored in your browser's local storage. Nothing is sent to a server. You can clear your history at any time using the clear button, and all data stays on your device.

Can I use keyboard shortcuts?

Absolutely. Type numbers and operators directly from your keyboard. Press Enter to evaluate, Backspace to delete the last character, and Escape to clear the display.
